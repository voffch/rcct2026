Karl K. Irikura received his undergraduate degree from Harvard College and his Ph.D. from the California Institute of Technology, studying experimental gas-phase ion chemistry with J. L. Beauchamp and electronic structure theory with W. A. Goddard III. He moved to the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) in 1991 to study experimental multiphoton ionization spectroscopy with J. W. Hudgens, then received a permanent position in computational chemistry at NIST in the Chemical Kinetics and Thermodynamics Division, directed by S. G. Lias. He served a few years as the Leader of the Computational Chemistry Group but has mostly been focused on his research goal, which is to find new and better ways to apply electronic structure theory to practical and interesting problems in chemistry. For example, whether ab initio ionization cross sections could improve the accuracy of thermochemical measurements made by Knudsen-cell mass spectrometry.
